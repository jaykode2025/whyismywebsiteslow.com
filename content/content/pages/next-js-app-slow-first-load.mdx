Hey, Next.js is powerful for building fast apps, but first-load performance (especially hydration and large bundles) can still be a pain point for many developers and site owners.

## Why First Loads Often Suffer
- Large JavaScript bundles
- Heavy server components or data fetching on initial render
- Missing proper code splitting or image optimization

## Real-World Impact
- Poor first-load times hurt **user experience scores** and SEO.
- Delays over 2-3 seconds increase bounce rates significantly.
- Many Next.js sites struggle with **Cumulative Layout Shift** and LCP on mobile.
